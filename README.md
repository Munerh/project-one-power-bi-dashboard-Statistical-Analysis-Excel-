# project 1
One of the oldest and most important cultural events in the Arab world, the Riyadh International Book Fair began with just 120 publishers at King Saud University in 1978 and has since expanded to include 2,000 publishers from more than 30 countries. 
The question this project asks is: What does the data actually show about the book fair era of transformation?

This project uses an actual historical dataset assembled from official sources to track the fair's development over almost 50 years. Data was assembled from the Saudi Press Agency, Saudipedia, Al-Riyadh newspaper, and Publishing Trends, covering visitor numbers, publisher participation, countries represented, cultural programming, and sales figures

The analysis is structured across three historical eras: the university years (1978–2006), the government expansion phase (2007–2017), and the Vision 2030 transformation (2018–present) , revealing how a university book exhibition became one of the Arab world's premier cultural destinations.

**Tools used: Power BI (interactive dashboard) · Microsoft Excel (summary dashboard)
